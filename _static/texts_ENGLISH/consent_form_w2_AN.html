Consent Form

Hello, how are you today? My name is _________. I work with a non-partisan research organization called Matrice360 Uganda. In collaboration with researchers from the University of Geneva, we are conducting historical study of peace and conflict in Uganda for example the Lord' Resistance Army (LRA) conflict of opposing the central government of Uganda by LRA in northern Uganda.

Some time ago you took part in the first wave of our study: thank you for doing so. Today is the second part of the study.

The study is part of an international research collaboration led by Prof. Simon Hug at the University of Geneva, Switzerland and in collaboration with Matrice360 Uganda headed by Mr. Samuel in Uganda, The same study is also being conducted in Colombia, Turkey and Thailand.

The general study aims to better understand the impact of the conflict on the social wellbeing of those directly and indirectly affected by the war and how their integration in to the community can be made better. The findings from the study will help inform future reconstruction and integration programs and policies to improve those affected by such conflicts. You have been referred to us as one of the directly affected person by the war from this location.

You will be asked a series of questions and to participate in short activities where you will be able to earn some money. There are no right or wrong answers; the best answer is the one that reflects your opinion most accurately. The questionnaire will take approximately 45 minutes. As a token of our appreciation for your time and effort, you will receive 10,000-UGX in addition to what you would have won from the activities at the end of the questionnaire, as well as an additional compensation based on the short activities.

For the purpose of our study, we will ask questions about your life experiences during the conflict time, and your opinions on the opposing groups, but all your responses shall be kept confidential.

This questionnaire is fully anonymous: your answers are stored securely and separately from your contact information, on a secure server at the University of Geneva, to prevent re-identification. They will be used anonymously for research purposes by accredited researchers.

You have the right to terminate your participation at any time and for any reason, without any negative consequences. No significant risks are expected. If you feel uncomfortable during or after the study, please inform us immediately.

If you have any questions, please contact the research team via email at cooperatingforpeace@unige.ch. You can also contact Matrice360 Samuel on the number o773232600. If you are interested in the results of this study, you may contact the team for a research report. Since the data is collected anonymously, no personal information will be available.

We do not anticipate any risks from participating. This study has been reviewed and approved by an accredited Ugandan Research Ethics Committee and the Uganda National Council for Science and Technology (UNCST).
